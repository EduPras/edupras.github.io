I am a recent Computer Engineering graduate from Federal Technological University of Paraná (UTFPR), seeking a Master's or Ph.D. position in AI and embedded systems.

At university, I participated in two main research projects: IoT Platform Management and Detecting endangered species with a Geospatial Foundation Model. I have also been awarded the MITACS GRA twice. The first research internship focused on CLIP-LoRA Optimization (INRS, Montréal), while the second, at Université Laval, Québec City, focuses on applying Machine Learning Algorithms in Sustainable Mining Engineering.

I am also an affiliated researcher at CEIA-UFG (the 3rd largest AI research center in the world) for over two years, bridging cutting-edge AI research and industry applications. I'm currently working as a Machine Learning Engineer for a client in the EdTech field, building a Knowledge Tracing system using Transductive Graph Learning to predict students' answers.

I am driven by the challenge of deploying powerful computational solutions on resource-constrained devices (Edge AI) and leveraging foundation models for real-world environmental applications. My goal is to pursue graduate research at the intersection of efficient model adaptation and geospatial intelligence.

Research & Projects

Remote sensing with Geospatial Foundation Model (GFM)

2025

Supervised by Prof. Daniel Cavalcanti (UTFPR) · Capstone project

This project uses Prithvi-EO-2.0 — a GFM created by NASA and IBM — to segment araucaria angustifolia (known as Araucaria) canopy. The dataset was obtained from Google Earth satellite imagery and annotated by a specialist in the field of botany. Even with only 30 ground truth images and 10 epochs of training, the model was able to identify not only the target canopies, but even the ones that went unnoticed.

PyTorch Foundation Models Computer Vision

CLIP-LoRA-XS

2024 — 2025

Supervised by Prof. Leszek Szczecinski (INRS) · Funded by MITACS GRA

During my research internship at Institut National de la Recherche Scientifique (INRS) in Montréal, I proposed an optimized fine-tuning solution for CLIP-LoRA, using the formula presented on the LoRA-XS paper, creating CLIP-LoRA-XS. The results show that even using half of the trainable parameters used by the original CLIP-LoRA, the model achieved similar results.

PyTorch LoRA Vision-Language Models

Sentilo IoT Platform

2022 — 2023

Supervised by Prof. Edson Tavares Camargo (UTFPR) · Funded by Araucaria Research Grant · Published →

As a Research Assistant, I created a containerized solution for Sentilo. The focus of the research was to create a cost-effective alternative solution to monitor the air quality, using low-cost sensors. I added layers to communicate with ElasticSearch, using Logstash to capture the data arriving on Redis. Later, this project was used to test the scalability.

Docker ElasticSearch Redis IoT

Stable diffusion from scratch

2023

Driven by curiosity about the mathematical foundations of image generation, I studied and implemented a simple rice grain generator with Stable Diffusion. Dataset used (only 5k per rice type): Rice Image Dataset.

Python Generative AI Mathematics

KinectV2 ROS2

2024

During one of my research projects, we needed to simulate a world using ROS2 and Gazebo with KinectV2. As the only model available at the time was KinectV1, I implemented the URDF model to use it on the simulations.
